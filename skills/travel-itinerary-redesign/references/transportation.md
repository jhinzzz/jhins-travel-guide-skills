# Transportation

## Purpose

Plan round-trip transport that is bookable, correctly timed, and honestly priced — never invent schedules or fares.

## Scope

Covers: flights, high-speed rail, conventional rail, long-distance bus, shuttle bus, airport/station express, ferry, taxi, ride-hailing, and rental car / self-drive.

## Evidence Standard

- Always research current schedules and prices at planning time using official sources or major OTAs. See [travel-sources.md](travel-sources.md) for the canonical source list and which platforms to use for each transport type.
- Never fabricate specific departure times, flight numbers, train numbers, or fares.
- If schedule or price data is unavailable, state the typical range and mark it as approximate.
- Prefer official booking channels: airline websites, 12306 (China rail), national rail sites, official bus company portals.
- Cross-check prices on at least two sources when the trip cost is significant.
- Cite the source and research date for every schedule and price claim, using the citation format in [travel-sources.md](travel-sources.md).

## Booking Window Guidelines

Use these as default recommendations. Adjust for peak seasons, holidays, and local conditions.

### Flights

- International: start monitoring 3 months ahead; book 6–8 weeks ahead for best prices.
- Domestic: start monitoring 6 weeks ahead; book 2–4 weeks ahead.
- Budget carriers: book as early as possible — prices only rise.
- Peak season or holidays: book as soon as tickets open; add 2–4 weeks to the normal lead time.

### High-Speed Rail / Intercity Rail

- China (12306): tickets release 15 days before departure. Book on release day for popular routes during holidays.
- Japan (JR): reserve up to 1 month ahead; JR Pass can be purchased in Japan but is cheaper when bought before arrival — recommend pre-purchase for cost savings.
- Europe (DB, SNCF, Trenitalia, Renfe): advance fares open 3–6 months ahead; best prices 2–3 months out.
- Taiwan (THSR): up to 28 days ahead; early-bird discounts available at 10%, 20%, or 35% off.
- South Korea (KTX): up to 1 month ahead via Korail.

### Conventional Rail

- Same booking windows as high-speed rail for the relevant country.
- Sleeper berths on popular routes sell out faster — book on release day.

### Long-Distance Bus

- Bookable 3–7 days ahead on most platforms.
- Holiday routes: book 1–2 weeks ahead.

### Shuttle Bus / Airport Express / Station Link

- Usually no advance booking required; frequency-based service.
- Check first and last service times — missing the last bus is a common trip failure.

### Ferry

- Popular island routes in peak season: book 1–2 weeks ahead.
- Off-peak: day-of booking is usually fine.

### Taxi / Ride-Hailing

- Real-time booking for urban trips.
- Pre-book airport or station transfers 1 day ahead for early-morning or late-night departures.
- Flag surge pricing risk during peak hours, rain, and holidays.

### Rental Car / Self-Drive

- Book 2–4 weeks ahead for standard destinations; 4–6 weeks for peak season or popular routes (e.g., Iceland Ring Road, Australian Great Ocean Road, US national parks).
- Confirm driver's license validity: International Driving Permit (IDP) required in many countries; Japan requires a specific JAF translation, not a standard IDP.
- Note: China does not recognize foreign or international driver's licenses — rental car is not a viable option for foreign visitors in mainland China.
- Include insurance options (CDW, liability, personal accident) in the price estimate.
- Check fuel type (petrol, diesel, EV charging network), road toll systems (ETC, vignette, toll-by-plate), and parking cost at the hotel.
- For one-way rentals (pick up city A, drop off city B), flag the drop-off surcharge — it can be significant.
- Return time is a hard deadline: late return typically incurs a full extra day charge.

## Recommended Arrival Times

These are the recommended times to arrive at the departure point before the scheduled departure. The "minimum" column is the absolute cutoff — arriving later risks missing the service.

### Flights

| Scenario | Recommended | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| International | 3 hours before departure | 2 hours |
| Domestic | 2 hours before departure | 1.5 hours |
| Peak season or congested airport | Add 30 minutes to above | — |

- Check-in counters typically close 45–60 minutes before departure.
- Boarding gates typically close 15–20 minutes before departure.
- For international arrivals, add immigration + baggage claim + customs time to the landing time before planning ground activities: typically 30–90 minutes depending on airport congestion, visa type, and whether the traveler has automated clearance (e.g., Global Entry, trusted traveller programs). Do not use "wheels down" as "available in the city".

### High-Speed Rail

| Scenario | Recommended | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Standard station | 30–45 minutes before departure | 15 minutes |
| Large hub with security screening (e.g., Beijing South, Shanghai Hongqiao, Guangzhou South) | 45–60 minutes | 20 minutes |

- In China, ticket check closes 5 minutes before departure — this is a hard gate closure, not a suggestion.
- During Spring Festival, National Day, and other peak holidays, add 15–30 minutes to the above for mega-hubs (e.g., Guangzhou South, Beijing West, Shanghai Hongqiao) due to security screening queues.

### Conventional Rail

| Scenario | Recommended | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 30–60 minutes before departure | 20 minutes |

### Long-Distance Bus

| Scenario | Recommended | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 20–30 minutes before departure | 10 minutes |

### Ferry

| Scenario | Recommended | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Passenger ferry | 30–60 minutes before departure | 20 minutes |
| Vehicle ferry | 60–90 minutes before departure | 45 minutes |

### Shuttle Bus / Airport Express

- Arrive 5–10 minutes before scheduled departure or per posted frequency.
- For the last service of the day: arrive 15 minutes early — late services are sometimes cancelled without notice.

## Price Guidance

- State prices as ranges when exact fares are not confirmed (e.g., "¥550–¥800" not "¥675").
- Always specify currency.
- Include class or seat type (economy, business, first class for flights; second class, first class for rail).
- Note factors that affect price: booking time, season, class, refundability.
- For taxis and ride-hailing, give estimated fare ranges and note surge pricing conditions.
- If comparing transport modes, present a price-vs-time comparison table so the user can make an informed tradeoff.
- Label all prices with the date or time window they were researched — fares change.

## Transfer Planning

When a trip involves connections (e.g., flight → metro → hotel, or train → shuttle → ferry):

- Map the full door-to-door chain, not just the main leg.
- Include transfer time between modes with a realistic buffer.
- Minimum connection buffer: 30 minutes for same-station transfers, 60 minutes for cross-station or cross-terminal transfers.
- Identify the riskiest link in the chain and flag it.
- Provide a backup plan for the riskiest link (e.g., "if the shuttle is missed, taxi costs approximately ¥XX and takes YY minutes").
- Account for luggage: transfers with heavy bags need more time than the map suggests.

## Return Trip Planning

- Plan the return trip with the same rigor as the outbound trip.
- Account for check-out times, luggage storage, and last-day activities when setting departure time.
- For flights: work backward from departure time → airport arrival → transit to airport → hotel check-out.
- For trains: work backward from departure → station arrival → transit to station → hotel check-out.
- Include a buffer for unexpected delays on the last day — do not schedule the return with zero slack.
- If the return is on the last day of a holiday, warn that transport may be crowded or sold out and recommend booking earlier.

## Output Format

For each transport leg, present:

1. **Mode and route**: e.g., "High-speed rail: Shanghai Hongqiao → Hangzhou East"
2. **Recommended booking window**: when to buy tickets
3. **Booking channel**: where to buy (e.g., 12306, airline website, Trip.com)
4. **Schedule or frequency**: specific departure times if researched, or service frequency if shuttle/bus
5. **Seat class and price range**: with currency and research date
6. **Recommended arrival time**: when to arrive at departure point
7. **Hard cutoff time**: the latest possible arrival without missing the service
8. **Transfer details**: how to get from the previous point to this departure point
9. **Backup option**: alternative mode or later service if this one is missed

Keep transport cards compact and scannable. Use tables or chip-style labels, not paragraphs.

## Non-Goals

- Do not invent specific flight numbers, train numbers, or exact departure times without evidence.
- Do not guarantee prices — always present as ranges or estimates and note the research date.
- Do not assume transport availability during strikes, typhoons, or service disruptions without checking.
- Do not skip the return trip or treat it as an afterthought.
- Do not recommend a transport mode without considering the full door-to-door time including transfers.
